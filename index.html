The Kinnaur alternative stays in the drawer as the weather fallback.

Why We're Staying the Night in Pooh

Three reasons. The gathering — the run converging on one village for a night is exactly the kind of thing you do this trip for. The driving — the old routes' worst feature was a 9–13 hour monster day from Tabo through Pooh to Reckong Peo, and the overnight splits it into two humane days: Maling Nala is no longer raced against a 3 PM deadline, and the Peo–Pooh cliff road gets driven fresh in the morning instead of at hour eleven. What it costs: the banked spare day (that's where it went), the Sissu and Chandratal decorations, and the Sarahan stop. Key Gompa survives inside the permit-processing window, Kalpa gets its evening, and Tabo keeps its monastery night. And third, since the night is a choice rather than a rule, it doubles as our only buffer: lose a day early and Days 4–5 can merge back into the old single monster day, trading the gathering for the schedule. Pooh itself is a basic-guesthouse village at 2,837 m in the Hangrang gateway — arrive with daylight and no expectations.

What this route honestly costs

  • Zero spare days on paper, but the Pooh night is the buffer of last resort: since it's suggested rather than required, a lost day can be recovered by merging Days 4–5 back into the old Tabo → Peo single day (9–13 h) — at the cost of the gathering. Day 5 short (4–6 h) and Day 3's half-day are the smaller shock absorbers.
  • Day 2 is still the crux: 9–11 hours over the worst 50 km of the trip (Gramphu–Batal: loose gravel, glacier fords, ~12 km/h), then the Kunzum switchbacks. No signal for 85 km; Batal has a satellite payphone.
  • Altitude ramp is one night at Chhatru (3,360 m), then over 4,551 m. Neal and Jibran carry Ladakh acclimatization; Abe arrives with none — Diamox from Sep 1 is non-negotiable.
  • Ends with two big hill days (Peo → Narkanda → Chandigarh, 9–11 h and 7–9 h) because Sarahan doesn't fit anymore.
  • Pooh accommodation is basic village guesthouses — identify one ahead if possible, arrive in daylight, expect little.

Why this is emergency-only now

  • Day 5 is a 9–12 hour drive that ends where it started, and Tapri–Wangtu (the Nigulsari collapse zone) gets crossed twice. Staying for the Pooh gathering instead would turn Days 6–7 into back-to-back 11–14 h monsters — that's the trade this route can't afford.
  • It still buys what it always bought: no Kunzum, no altitude (highest sleep 2,837 m), fuel and signal throughout. If the pass is shut, this gets the team to Jaisalmer.
  • The decision point is the Gramphu junction, ~2.5 h after launch — or ideally the night before, from BRO status and local drivers at the Sep 1 briefing. Retreating from Gramphu costs about half a day; the fallback absorbs it by trimming Day 2 short of Shimla and pushing Day 3.
  • Standing question for the Adventurists if invoked: the Manali → Mandi leg trends south — flag it as the forced fallback and get a blessing in writing.
