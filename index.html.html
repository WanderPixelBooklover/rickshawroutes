The Shape of the Problem: the Chandigarh Gate

With the Jaipur rest day fixed, the plains block is identical and immovable on every route: Chandigarh to Shekhawati on Sep 9, Jaipur on Sep 10, rest on Sep 11, Jodhpur on Sep 12, Jaisalmer on Sep 13. Chandigarh–Jaipur is ~510 km and needs its overnight split no matter what, and Jaipur–Jaisalmer needs its Jodhpur night. Nothing in Rajasthan compresses.

Which means every route gets exactly seven mountain days, Sep 2–8, and must sleep in Chandigarh — or Kasauli, 60 km short of it — on the night of Tue Sep 8. The routes don't differ in how much mountain time they get. They differ in how they spend it.

What this route honestly costs

  • Day 2 is 9–11 hours over loose gravel, glacier fords, and the Kunzum switchbacks, with no tar for 50 km and no mobile signal for 85 km (Batal has a satellite payphone). At altitude the rickshaw makes roughly 12 km/h on this surface.
  • Almost no acclimatization ramp: one night at Chhatru (3,360 m), then over 4,551 m. Neal and Jibran carry acclimatization from the Ladakh trek; Abe arrives with none — Diamox from Sep 1 is non-negotiable, and his headache is the schedule's biggest single risk here.
  • The spare day is real but earned by driving the same road as Route 2 and stopping less. If nothing goes wrong, you reach Chandigarh with a full day banked — spendable anywhere.
  • Fallback is built in: if Kunzum or Gramphu–Batal is shut on Sep 3, backtrack through the tunnel and adopt the Route 3 line, spending the spare day on the retreat. Route 1 is the only route that can absorb its own Plan B.

The one-day fork (pick one, not both)

  • Altitude-safe version (table above): Sissu night + Chandratal evening + Key Gompa afternoon. Sleeping profile Manali 2,050 → Sissu 3,120 → Batal 3,960 → Kaza 3,650 is a textbook ramp — the safest possible plan for an unacclimatized Abe.
  • Day-trip version: drop the Sissu night, run Day 1 to Chhatru and Day 2 straight to Kaza (Route 1's hard Day 2), and gain a full day at Kaza on Sep 4 — Kibber, Chicham Bridge, and Langza, or the Langza–Hikkim–Komik loop. One cluster fits in a day at rickshaw pace; two don't.

What this route honestly costs

  • Zero spare days — the exploration day is the buffer. Lose a day to weather or a breakdown and the side-trips vanish with nothing else lost; lose two and the Jaipur rest day goes.
  • It ends with two consecutive 9–11 hour hill days (Peo → Narkanda → Chandigarh), because the gentler three-day descent doesn't fit. The reward for the calm start is a hard finish.
  • The Kunzum wildcard applies exactly as on Route 1, but Route 2 meets it a day later with less slack: a forced retreat to the Kinnaur line costs the entire identity of the route.

What this route honestly costs

  • The "safe" road is only safe from snow and altitude. During an active monsoon it doubles exposure to the two worst landslide sections in Himachal: Tapri–Wangtu twice (a 70 m stretch of this highway collapsed at Nigulsari in September 2024) and the Peo–Pooh cliff road twice in a single day.
  • No Spiti. The Nako–Tabo–Kaza out-and-back needs a day the schedule doesn't have — within Sep 2–13 it only fits by cutting the Jaipur rest day, which is fixed. Pooh is the turnaround.
  • Longest route (~2,130 km vs ~1,900), with three of seven mountain days at 9+ hours, and Day 5 is a 9–12 hour drive that ends where it started.
  • Rules check needed: the Manali → Mandi leg trends south. Confirm with the Adventurists that the Shimla-first line satisfies "any direction except south" — the Pooh checkpoint is presumably the actual control, but ask before betting the route on it.
  • What it buys: zero AMS risk for anyone, reliable fuel the whole way, mobile signal almost throughout, hospitals within reach, and immunity to whatever Kunzum is doing that morning.

The Decision

All three are legitimate; none is free. The real decision point is late: Kunzum's condition can't be known until the Sep 1 briefing in Manali, and the 2026 monsoon's temper won't be clear until late August. So the practical move is to prepare Routes 1 and 2 as one plan with a fork, hold Route 3 as the standing fallback, and decide at the Gramphu junction if it comes to that.

PickIf
Route 1Kunzum reports clean on Sep 1 and we value margin over lingering — the only route where a breakdown doesn't immediately eat something we care about.
Route 2The mountains are the point and we accept that a single lost day erases the side-trips. Choose the Sissu version for Abe's lungs, the day-trip version if he lands early enough to acclimatize in Manali.
Route 3The 2026 monsoon is still violent by late August, Kunzum looks doubtful, or anyone's altitude tolerance is a worry. Also the default retreat if Routes 1/2 hit a wall at Gramphu.
